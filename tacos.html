Fish Tacos

Flavorful Mexican-style tacos with broiled fish and fresh toppings.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb firm white fish (tilapia, halibut, or mahi mahi)
  • 2 teaspoons Mexican chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¬Ω teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 8 small whole wheat tortillas
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt or sour cream
  • 1 cup fresh salsa
  • ¬Ω cup microgreens
  • ¬º cup minced green onions
  • ¬º cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Equipment

  • Rimmed baking sheet
  • Aluminum foil
  • Mixing bowl
  • Chef's knife
  • Cutting board
  • Cooking spray

To Serve

  • Extra lime wedges
  • Hot sauce
  • Avocado slices
  • Shredded cabbage
  • Mexican beer or margaritas

Tip: For extra flavor, add 1 teaspoon ground cumin to the spice mix.

Instructions

Step 1.

Set oven rack in upper third and preheat broiler to high. Line baking sheet with aluminum foil and lightly coat with olive oil.

Step 2.

In a small bowl, combine chili powder, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Pat fish dry and cut into 1-inch wide strips.

Step 3.

Rub spice mixture evenly over all sides of fish pieces. Arrange on prepared baking sheet and lightly spray with cooking spray.

Step 4.

Broil fish for 5-7 minutes until cooked through and lightly charred in spots (time will vary based on fish thickness).

Step 5.

While fish cooks, warm tortillas according to package directions. Prepare all toppings.

Step 6.

To assemble: Spread each tortilla with 1 tablespoon yogurt or sour cream. Add 2-3 pieces of fish, then top with salsa, microgreens, green onions, and cilantro.

Step 7.

Serve immediately with lime wedges for squeezing over tacos.
