Spaghetti Carbonara

Classic Roman pasta dish with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 8 ounces pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¬º cup dry white wine
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • ¬Ω cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Equipment

  • Large pot
  • Large skillet
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Tongs
  • Colander

To Serve

  • Extra grated cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Crusty bread
  • Green salad
  • Dry white wine

Tip: Use the pasta cooking water to help create the creamy sauce.

Instructions

Step 1.

Bring large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente, about 10-12 minutes. Reserve 1 cup pasta water, then drain and toss with 1 tbsp olive oil.

Step 2.

While pasta cooks, heat remaining oil in large skillet. Add pancetta and cook until crisp. Remove with slotted spoon, leaving fat in pan.

Step 3.

Add onion to skillet and cook until soft, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more. Deglaze with wine, scraping up browned bits.

Step 4.

In bowl, whisk eggs with cheeses and pepper. Slowly whisk in ¬Ω cup reserved pasta water to temper eggs.

Step 5.

Return cooked pancetta to skillet. Add drained pasta and toss to coat. Remove from heat.

Step 6.

Quickly stir in egg mixture, tossing constantly until sauce thickens. Add more pasta water if needed.

Step 7.

Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with parsley and serve immediately.
